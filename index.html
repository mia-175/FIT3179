Human Development Index (HDI) 2022

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a summary measure of three key dimensions of human development: standard of living (income),
education and health.

These are measured by gross national income (GNI) per capita, mean years of schooling, and life expectancy at birth (respectively).

This visualisation aims to explore the key dimensions of human development of countries around the world; in particular the differences between affluent nations and those with limited economic resources. To do this, the top 10 highest and lowest scoring countries in each of the three dimensions are compared.

In 2022, Switzerland had the highest HDI (0.967), and Australia the 10th highest (0.946). The country with the lowest HDI was Somalia (0.380).

Gross National Income (NGI) per capita is a measure of income per person. The vast difference between the wealthiest and poorest countries is clear, with the top 10 countries having NGI per capita values over $100,000, and the bottom 10 countries having values below $1,000.

For lower income countries, the actual (mean) years of schooling is significantly lower than expected, whereas for higher income countries the actual (mean) years of schooling is very close to the expected value.

The average person from Hong Kong lives lives 30 years longer than someone born in Chad. A common pattern across all countries is that Females tend to live longer than males.
